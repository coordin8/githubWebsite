Backstory

Oddly enough, Computer Science was not my major in College; it was Mechanical Engineering. See I grew up working on cars and dirt bikes so I thought Mechanical Engineering was the appropriate major for me. This all changed when I took a course on Matlab in my third year as a Mechanical Engineering student. The final project for this class was to create anything you want in Matlab just as long as it was challenging. I jumped on this opportunity and created a 3D simulation of a racecar driving around a track doing flips over obstacles, drifts around turns, and wheelies here and there. This project actually got top in class and got me a bonus of 5% extra credit towards my total grade but I did not care about that. I created that program because it was fun! For my next big project, I decided to create a letter recognition software program even though I had no clue what computer vision was. When I was finished I realized something.

I was addicted to programming...

And so I took an introductory course on c++ in what should have been my final quarter at UCR. At that point, I had a choice between graduating or pursuing a minor in Computer Science. Midway though the quarter it was obvious I had to get a minor. Following that quarter, I took nothing but Computer Science courses straight till I had a minor. Now I understand that most companies might be looking for someone with more expereince than a minor so in whatever free time I have, I develop. I have created some pretty awesome programs. Currently, I am developing a game for iOS that is very similar to geometry wars. I plan on publishing it for free when I am done to get my name out there. Check out my projects page for more projects I have done.
